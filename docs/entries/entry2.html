Recap on the flight
and the first
impressions

From memory

Posted on June 23, 2019

A little over four months have passed since I left Germany. Looking back at the departure from Munich airport to landing in Brisbane everything went as planned. The take off from Munich was on schedule at 10pm and the flight restful to the amount possible. The picture shows the moment the plane loses contact with the tarmac and the journey begins.

Departure from Munich in the middle of the night

Dubai with its concept of being a "silent airport" is a interesting place to come by. It is well maintained and the palm trees make for a relaxing atmosphere which I have never experienced before. The layover time was around 3:20h. The watchful observant can notice that aggregating the travel and layover time the night has passed from the perspective of the Munich clock. Under this circumstance the "silent" property of the airport proved to be more of a burden then a plus. Sitting in front of a gate, never being able to close the eyes or snooze is painful. Especially the moment looking up at the display panels after being distracted makes for some nice chills. However the crown jewel of the flight are infants on a 16h flight. I have never been so thankful in my life for noise cancelling headphones.

Layover in Dubai

Arriving early in the morning in the Australian summer heat was amazing. Feeling the sunshine on the skin and smelling wet grass is great after leaving form the winter shaken Bavarian capital. By the way: The Hoody is resting peacefully in the closet ever since.
